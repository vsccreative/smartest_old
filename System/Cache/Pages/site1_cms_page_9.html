          Multi-mineral strategy focused on diamonds, gold, base metals, and uranium

          Savannah pursues a multi-mineral strategy, focused on the discovery and development of primary diamond deposits in addition to gold, base metals and uranium. Success is being achieved in exploring a vast expanse of untested territory in central Tanzania by applying innovative thinking and modern techniques.

          The central region of Tanzania is divided by a major north-south watershed.  The development of streams and rivers to the east is markedly more pronounced than that to the west and, using digital elevation modelling, it can be established that the west is an area of flat terrain whereas the east is heavily dissected by watercourses.

          In terms of kimberlite exploration, the sole method available in the 1950's was the collection of sediment samples to recover Kimberlite Indicator Minerals (KIM's). These would have been released into sediments where kimberlites were exposed by erosion i.e. to the east of the watershed.  

          Limited kimberlite exploration in the 1990's, centred on the sixty-year old diamond mine, Mwadui, utilising geophysical techniques and refined sampling methods, resulted in the discovery of approximately 100 more kimberlites.  By inference, other areas which were known to host kimberlites and, more significantly, areas which would have produced few sample results in the 1950's and 1960's are therefore considered to be capable of containing not only new kimberlites but new kimberlite provinces.

          There has been little exploration for commodities other than diamonds, with the exception of the Lake Victoria Goldfield and areas around Mbeya to the south.  The lack of rock outcrop in much of the central region has precluded all but very large-scale geological mapping.  The general increase in knowledge of different styles of mineralisation since the 1950's allows for a much better interpretation of the results of geochemical sampling and geological structure derived from geophysical sources.
